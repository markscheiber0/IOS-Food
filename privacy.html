Food Log — Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 15, 2026

Food Log ("the app") is a calorie and nutrition tracking app operated by Mark Scheiber. This policy explains what data the app collects and how it is used.

What we collect

  • Account data. Your email address (or Apple ID credential if you use Sign in with Apple) is used solely to create and secure your account.
  • Food log data. The meals you log — descriptions, estimated calories and macronutrients, and timestamps — are stored so the app can show your history, totals, and trends. This is health and fitness data linked to your account.
  • Daily goal. Your calorie goal setting.

How your data is processed

  • Storage & authentication: account and food log data are stored with Supabase. Row-level security ensures only you can read your own data.
  • AI nutrition estimates: when you log a meal, the text description you provide is sent to Anthropic's API to estimate nutrition values. Only the food description is sent — never your name, email, or account identifiers.

What we don't do

  • We do not sell your data.
  • We do not use your data for advertising or cross-app tracking.
  • We do not share your data with third parties beyond the processors listed above.

Siri shortcut

The optional "Log Food" Siri shortcut sends your spoken food description, along with a private token generated in the app, directly to our logging service. Voice recognition itself is handled by Apple under Apple's privacy terms.

Data deletion

You can permanently delete your account and all associated data at any time in the app: Settings → Delete account & all data. Deletion is immediate and irreversible.

Not medical advice

Nutrition values are approximate AI estimates intended for general wellness tracking. Food Log is not a medical device and does not provide medical or dietary advice.
